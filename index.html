Finding 1 - Core Result
A 1 ppt increase in supply-driven inflation widens the Q1-Q5 gap by 0.613 ppts. The same demand increase widens it by only 0.326 ppts. Supply shocks are 1.88x more regressive.
Finding 2 - Near-Zero Demand Effect
In demand-dominant months (N=115), the gap averages +0.025 ppts. In supply-dominant months (N=185): +0.579 ppts. T-test: t=-6.32, p<0.001.
Finding 3 - COVID Structural Break
Both coefficients shifted after Jan 2020 (Chow F=127.45). Supply fell 0.809 to 0.522; demand fell 0.644 to 0.248.
Finding 4 - Post-COVID Worst on Record
The 2021-2023 period: mean gap +0.806 ppts - the largest in the 25-year sample.
